Health Impact Summary

This page summarizes the potential health effects associated with different air quality levels and environmental conditions monitored by the BreathIQ system.

🟢 Good (0–50)

Air quality is considered safe. No significant health impacts are expected for the general population.

🟢 Satisfactory (51–100)

Acceptable air quality. Sensitive individuals may experience minor irritation after prolonged exposure.

🟡 Moderate (101–200)

Individuals with respiratory conditions may experience discomfort, headaches, fatigue, or mild breathing difficulty.

🟠 Poor (201–300)

Prolonged exposure may lead to coughing, chest irritation, breathing discomfort, and reduced concentration.

🔴 Very Poor (301–400)

Significant respiratory stress may occur. Vulnerable populations should reduce exposure and improve ventilation.

🟣 Severe (401–500)

Hazardous conditions. Immediate action is recommended to reduce exposure and improve air quality.

Sensor Health Impact Overview

CO₂ Levels

Elevated CO₂ can contribute to drowsiness, headaches, reduced concentration, and fatigue.

VOC Levels

High VOC concentrations may irritate the eyes, nose, and throat while affecting indoor comfort.

Dust Particles

Fine particles can enter deep into the lungs and contribute to respiratory irritation and inflammation.

Temperature

Extreme temperatures can increase physical stress and affect overall comfort and wellbeing.

Humidity

Low humidity may dry the respiratory tract, while high humidity can promote mold and allergen growth.

Oxygen Levels

Reduced oxygen levels can impact breathing efficiency, alertness, and physical performance.
