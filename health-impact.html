🟢 Good Air Quality (0–50)

Health Impact

Air quality is considered excellent and poses little or no risk to human health.

Possible Effects

  • No expected respiratory symptoms
  • Optimal oxygen exchange efficiency
  • Comfortable breathing during exercise
  • No measurable cardiovascular stress
  • Normal cognitive performance

Long-Term Outlook

No significant adverse effects are expected even with prolonged exposure.

Recommended Action

Normal activity can continue without restrictions.

🟢 Satisfactory Air Quality (51–100)

Health Impact

Generally safe for the majority of the population.

Possible Symptoms

  • Mild throat dryness
  • Occasional nasal irritation
  • Minor discomfort in sensitive individuals
  • Slight allergic reactions

Body Response

Minor airway irritation may occur in individuals with existing respiratory conditions.

Long-Term Outlook

No permanent health damage is expected under normal exposure.

Recommended Action

Maintain good ventilation and continue normal activities.

🟡 Moderate Air Quality (101–200)

Health Impact

Sensitive individuals may begin experiencing respiratory discomfort.

Possible Symptoms

  • Dry throat
  • Eye irritation
  • Mild headaches
  • Reduced concentration
  • Fatigue
  • Occasional coughing

Physiological Effects

  • Mild airway inflammation
  • Increased mucus production
  • Reduced breathing comfort
  • Increased respiratory sensitivity

Long-Term Risks

  • More frequent asthma symptoms
  • Reduced respiratory comfort
  • Increased sensitivity to pollutants

Recommended Action

Improve ventilation and reduce prolonged exposure for sensitive groups.

🟠 Poor Air Quality (201–300)

Health Impact

Air quality may affect even healthy individuals after prolonged exposure.

Possible Symptoms

  • Persistent coughing
  • Chest discomfort
  • Breathing difficulty during exercise
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches
  • Reduced productivity

Physiological Effects

  • Reduced oxygen transfer efficiency
  • Respiratory stress
  • Increased cardiovascular workload
  • Early inflammatory response

Long-Term Risks

  • Chronic airway irritation
  • Reduced lung efficiency
  • Aggravated asthma conditions
  • Increased respiratory infections

Recommended Action

Limit exposure, improve air circulation and avoid strenuous activity.

🔴 Very Poor Air Quality (301–400)

Health Impact

Health effects become likely for everyone, especially vulnerable groups.

Possible Symptoms

  • Breathing difficulty
  • Chest tightness
  • Severe coughing
  • Dizziness
  • Eye irritation
  • Persistent headaches

Physiological Effects

  • Significant respiratory inflammation
  • Reduced lung performance
  • Elevated heart workload
  • Reduced oxygen utilization

Long-Term Risks

  • Accelerated respiratory decline
  • Frequent asthma attacks
  • Increased cardiovascular risk
  • Persistent airway damage

Recommended Action

Stay indoors when possible and use air filtration systems.

🟣 Severe Air Quality (401–500)

Health Impact

Hazardous environmental conditions. Serious health effects are possible.

Possible Symptoms

  • Severe respiratory distress
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Persistent chest pain
  • Dizziness and confusion
  • Shortness of breath
  • Potential hypoxia

Physiological Effects

  • Severe reduction in oxygen uptake
  • Significant cardiovascular strain
  • Acute respiratory inflammation
  • Potential medical emergency

Long-Term Risks

  • Permanent respiratory damage
  • Cardiovascular complications
  • Increased hospitalization risk
  • Severe deterioration in vulnerable individuals

Recommended Action

Immediate intervention required. Leave the affected environment if possible and seek medical attention if symptoms occur.

This analysis is intended for educational and environmental awareness purposes only. Actual health outcomes depend on exposure duration, pollutant concentration, individual sensitivity and existing medical conditions.
