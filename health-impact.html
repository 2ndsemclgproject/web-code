Health Impact Analysis

Understand how prolonged exposure to different air quality conditions can affect the respiratory system, cardiovascular health, oxygen exchange efficiency, cognitive performance and overall wellbeing.

🟢 Excellent Air Quality (BQI 81–100)

Respiratory Health

Air quality is considered optimal. Pollutant concentrations are sufficiently low that the lungs can perform oxygen exchange efficiently without measurable respiratory stress.

Benefits

  • Efficient oxygen absorption
  • Lower respiratory irritation
  • Improved exercise performance
  • Reduced airway inflammation risk
  • Better sleep quality

Long-Term Exposure

No significant health concerns are expected even during prolonged exposure. This environment is ideal for children, elderly individuals, athletes and people with respiratory conditions.

🔵 Good Air Quality (BQI 61–80)

Respiratory Effects

Air quality remains acceptable for most individuals. However, prolonged exposure may cause mild irritation in sensitive groups.

Possible Symptoms

  • Mild throat irritation
  • Occasional nasal discomfort
  • Minor allergic reactions
  • Slight breathing discomfort during intense activity

Long-Term Impact

Generally safe. Individuals with asthma or allergies may experience intermittent symptoms after extended exposure.

🟠 Moderate Air Quality (BQI 41–60)

Immediate Effects

  • Dry throat
  • Eye irritation
  • Headaches
  • Reduced concentration
  • Fatigue
  • Mild coughing

What Happens Inside The Body

Airborne pollutants begin interacting with the respiratory tract and bronchial passages. The body's immune system may respond by increasing mucus production and triggering low-level inflammation.

Long-Term Exposure Risks

  • Chronic respiratory irritation
  • Reduced lung efficiency
  • Increased sensitivity to allergens
  • Higher frequency of asthma symptoms

High-Risk Groups

  • Children
  • Elderly individuals
  • Pregnant women
  • Asthma patients
  • COPD patients

🔴 Poor Air Quality (BQI 0–40)

Immediate Health Effects

  • Persistent coughing
  • Chest tightness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Eye irritation
  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches

Respiratory Impact

High pollutant concentrations can inflame the airways, reduce oxygen exchange efficiency and place significant stress on lung tissue. Breathing may become more difficult, particularly during physical activity.

Cardiovascular Effects

  • Increased blood pressure
  • Higher cardiac workload
  • Elevated heart stress
  • Potential cardiovascular complications

Neurological Effects

  • Mental fatigue
  • Reduced focus
  • Memory impairment
  • Mood disturbances
  • Reduced productivity

Long-Term Consequences

  • Chronic bronchitis
  • Reduced lung capacity
  • Worsening asthma symptoms
  • Persistent airway inflammation
  • Cardiovascular disease risk

Recommended Action

  • Improve ventilation immediately
  • Reduce exposure time
  • Use filtration masks when necessary
  • Seek medical attention if symptoms persist

Pollutant Breakdown

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

Elevated CO₂ levels can contribute to drowsiness, headaches, reduced cognitive performance and fatigue. Extremely high levels may impair decision-making and alertness.

Volatile Organic Compounds (VOC)

Prolonged exposure may result in eye irritation, respiratory discomfort, headaches, nausea and long-term health concerns depending on concentration and chemical composition.

Humidity

Low humidity can dry the skin and respiratory tract. High humidity encourages mold growth, allergen proliferation and breathing discomfort.

Temperature

Extreme temperatures increase physiological stress, reduce comfort and can place additional strain on respiratory and cardiovascular systems.

This analysis is intended for educational and environmental awareness purposes. Actual health outcomes depend on exposure duration, pollutant concentration, individual sensitivity and existing medical conditions.
